What is a Bode Plot, and why is it useful?

A plot that shows the frequency response of a system. Through a Bode Plot we can understand the amplitude of system using the dB scale (log-based) as well as the phase of the system, measured in degrees.
The Bode Plot can give designers a sense the amount of gain (dB) they can expect from a system at various frequencies. As well as how much bandwidth they can expect from a system. The phase plots can help designers detect instability.

What is a Transfer Function?

A Transfer function is a ratio of the output to the input of a system. It's a complex number that describes the amplitude as well as the phase of a system as a function of frequency.

How does a Bode Plot relate to a Low Pass Filter?

We can use an op-amp to filter out certain frequency ranges. As the name suggests, a low-pass filter, filters out frequencies at high frequencies (frequencies beyond the cutoff frequency) and allows frequencies lower than the cutoff frequency to pass through with minimal attenuation.
